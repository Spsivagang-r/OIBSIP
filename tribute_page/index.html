Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India & People's President

See timeline

About

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931 – 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Widely known as the "Missile Man of India", he played a pivotal role in India’s civilian space programme and military missile development.

Scientist

Key figure in ISRO and DRDO; led India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.

President

Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007) and was fondly called the “People’s President”.

Teacher

Even after presidency, he continued to inspire students across the country with his lectures and books.

Timeline

  1. 1931 – Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  2. 1960 – Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  3. 1969 – Joined ISRO; Project Director of India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  4. 1980s–90s – Played key role in development of ballistic missiles (Agni, Prithvi).
  5. 1998 – Instrumental in India’s nuclear tests at Pokhran-II.
  6. 2002 – Became the 11th President of India.
  7. 2015 – Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
